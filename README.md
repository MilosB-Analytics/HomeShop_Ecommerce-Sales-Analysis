## Project Overview
This project analyzes global e-commerce sales data using **Power Bi** to evaluate sales performance, customer demographics, product category trends, and year-over-year growth.

The goal of the analysis is to identify **key revenue drivers, customer segments, and product categories** contributing to overall business performance.

The analysis highlights **strong year-over-year sales performance**, with revenue steadily increasing throughout the year and peaking in **December with over 530K in sales**. Sales trends indicate a seasonal pattern, with growth accelerating in **Q4**, suggesting strong holiday-driven demand.

Customer demographic analysis shows that **Adults generate the majority of sales (~2.18M)**, significantly outperforming other age groups. **Young Adults (~1.04M)** and **Middle Age customers (~0.91M)** represent the next largest segments, while **Teenagers and Senior Citizens contribute a smaller share of total revenue**.

Product category performance reveals that **Electronics is the dominant revenue driver (~1.25M)**, followed by **Jewelry (~631K)** and **Automotive (~368K)**. Several mid-tier categories such as **Home & Garden, Sports, and Clothing** contribute moderate sales volume, while categories like **Books and Food & Grocery generate comparatively lower revenue**.

Geographic analysis indicates that the **United States is the company’s largest market**, significantly outperforming other countries. Secondary markets include the **United Kingdom, Germany, Canada, and Australia**, which contribute steady but smaller sales volumes.

Overall, the analysis demonstrates that the company’s growth is driven primarily by **strong US market performance, high-value product categories such as Electronics, and purchasing activity from adult customer segments**.

## Key Insights

### Customer Demographics

Customer purchasing behavior shows a clear concentration in older age groups.

- **Adults are the dominant revenue contributors (~2.18M)**, accounting for the largest share of total sales.
- **Young Adults generate ~1.04M in revenue**, making them the second most valuable customer segment.
- **Middle Age customers contribute ~0.91M**, indicating stable purchasing behavior across this demographic.
- **Teenagers (~0.5M)** and **Senior Citizens (~0.06M)** represent the smallest share of revenue.

### Monthly Sales Performance (Current vs Previous Year)

Sales trends indicate **steady growth throughout the year**, with noticeable seasonal patterns.

- **January begins strong (~348K)** before dipping slightly in **February (~299K)**.
- Sales gradually recover through **spring and summer**, stabilizing between **370K–393K**.
- Growth accelerates in **Q4**, with a sharp increase beginning in **October (~421K)**.
- **December records the highest revenue (~532K)**, indicating strong holiday season demand.

The **current year slightly outperforms the previous year in most months**, signaling consistent growth.

### Product Category Performance (2023 vs 2024)

Category analysis comparing **2023 and 2024 sales performance** reveals consistent product leaders as well as several seasonal shifts in category demand.

#### Category Leaders

The top-performing product categories remain **consistent across both years**, indicating stable customer demand.

| Category | Performance Insight |
|--------|--------|
| Electronics | Highest revenue generating category in both years |
| Jewelry | Strong and consistent secondary revenue driver |
| Automotive | Maintains a solid position among the top categories |

These categories continue to generate the largest share of total sales, suggesting they are **core revenue drivers for the business**.



---

#### Mid-Tier Category Competition

A noticeable dynamic appears between **Sports and Clothing categories** when comparing seasonal performance.

- **Clothing outperforms Sports during the first half of the year (Q1–Q2)**.
- **Sports surpasses Clothing during the second half of the year (Q3–Q4)**.

This shift suggests **seasonal consumer demand patterns**, potentially influenced by:

- Warmer weather and outdoor activities increasing sports-related purchases
- Seasonal apparel purchasing cycles earlier in the year



---

#### Year-over-Year Sales Comparison

While category rankings remain stable, there are noticeable differences between the two years.

- **2023 shows higher peak sales compared to 2024**, indicating stronger overall category performance during peak months.
- **2024 sales remain slightly lower but follow a similar trend pattern**, suggesting stable demand but slightly reduced peak activity.



---

#### Seasonal Performance Patterns

Both years demonstrate **similar seasonal behavior across product categories**.

- **Q1–Q2:** Clothing and general consumer goods perform steadily.
- **Q3–Q4:** Overall category sales increase, with **Sports and high-demand product segments gaining momentum**.
- The **strongest sales periods occur during Q3 and Q4**, aligning with seasonal demand and year-end shopping periods.

### Geographic Sales Distribution

Revenue is heavily concentrated in a few geographic markets.

- **United States is the largest market by a wide margin**, generating the highest share of total sales.
- Secondary markets include:
  - **United Kingdom**
  - **Germany**
  - **Canada**
  - **Australia**
- Smaller but emerging markets include:
  - **France**
  - **India**
  - **Japan**

💡 **Insight:** The company relies heavily on the **US market**, while other international markets contribute moderate but steady revenue streams.

---

### Strategic Takeaways

The analysis highlights several key business opportunities:

- Prioritize **US market retention and expansion**.
- Focus marketing efforts on **Adult and Young Adult customers**, who generate the majority of revenue.
- Leverage **seasonal demand spikes in Q4** with targeted promotions and inventory planning.
- Continue prioritizing **Electronics and Jewelry**, as they remain the strongest revenue generators.
- Plan **inventory and marketing strategies around Q3–Q4 demand peaks**.
- Leverage the **seasonal strength of Sports products in later quarters**.
- Optimize **Clothing promotions earlier in the year (Q1–Q2)** to align with demand cycles.
